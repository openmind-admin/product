I. The Challenge
Building a Field and Competing in One
The problem is multilevel — individual, institutional, and external factors all interact — and there is no known, reliably effective institutional model for creating and sustaining a culture of constructive dialogue. Competitors exist, but this is an emerging category most people don't know about yet.
No category
Nobody sells "campus culture change." Competitors own fragments — nobody holds the integrative frame.
No standard buyer
Provosts, student affairs VPs, state systems. The offering must flex across all of them.
No proven ROI
No industry benchmark. We must build the measurement and define what "success" means.
The implication: Our offering strategy can't follow conventional playbooks. We need a framework that organizes what we build, how we sell it, and how partners navigate it — all at once. That framework has two dimensions.

Pillar 5 · Measurement and Organizational Learning
The Biggest Gap — and Biggest Opportunity
Funders have moved from requesting measurement to requiring it. CDI tells partners to measure their dialogue culture but provides no tools to do so. This is both our most urgent gap and our most exciting innovation space.
Perspectives Evidence Base — Internal research on our own offerings. Not partner-facing.
Program-Specific Surveys — Each program measured separately. No unified view.
THE OPPORTUNITY
LLMs create an entirely new paradigm for measuring dialogue. Instead of static surveys and numeric scoring, the assessor can be a conversational AI — one that participates in and analyzes dialogue, adapts to context, generates narrative feedback, and can't be gamed the way traditional assessments can.

What better way to measure dialogue than through conversation itself?

This connects to:
● Generative assessment — augments traditional measures with reflective narrative [EXPLORATORY — needs alignment with Research]
● Measurement Learning Lab — partner-facing dashboard, benchmarking consortium
● Administrative data proxies — institutional data that already exists:
● · Roommate change requests
● · Speaker cancellations
● · Cross-group participation
This is where CDI can differentiate. Interfaith America, FIRE, and COFHE-style consortia are building traditional measurement. CDI has the opportunity to build something fundamentally different — measurement that embodies the dialogue it measures. The generative assessment concept is early-stage and requires alignment with the Research team before committing to a roadmap.

Personalized Practice
Personalized, reflective feedback embedded directly inside Perspectives activities. The AI mirrors and questions — it never evaluates, ranks, or labels.
Perspectives · Module 4: Constructive Disagreement · Post-Activity Coaching
CDI Coach
In the immigration dialogue, you asked two clarifying questions when the topic was abstract — but shifted to advocacy when it became personal. What do you notice about that pattern?
Student
I guess I get defensive when things feel close to my own experience
CDI Coach
That's a really honest observation. Many people find that proximity makes curiosity harder. Looking at your response in the exercise, what's one question you could have asked instead of the statement you made?
Student
Maybe "what shaped your view on this?" instead of "that's not how it works"
CDI Coach
That's a powerful reframe — it invites the other person's story rather than challenging their conclusion. This is something you can practice in your next in-person discussion. Notice when proximity triggers advocacy, and try one curiosity question before responding.
The impact: Every learner gets individualized reflection, not just content consumption. At scale, it's the difference between a course and a coaching experience. The AI does what a facilitator would do in a small group — but for every student, asynchronously, at the moment of learning.

Instead of "You scored 7/10 on perspective-taking," learners receive narrative reflections that surface patterns and generate questions. The assessment models what it measures.
Traditional: Numeric Scoring
Intellectual Humility: 6.2/10
Perspective-Taking: 7.8/10
Open-Mindedness: 5.4/10
Constructive Disagreement: 6.9/10

Overall: Above average. Consider practicing active listening.
Augmented: Generative Narrative
Across your Perspectives journey, a pattern emerged: you engage deeply and curiously when topics are abstract or unfamiliar — asking genuine questions, holding uncertainty well. But when topics connect to your personal experience, you tend to shift into advocacy earlier.

This isn't a flaw — it's a signal that proximity matters for you. The question worth sitting with: What would it look like to bring the same curiosity you show strangers' perspectives to the perspectives closest to your own?
The impact: Numeric scores flatten the very thing we're trying to develop. A "6.2 in intellectual humility" doesn't help anyone grow. A generative narrative that names a specific pattern and asks a question is the practice of dialogue — the assessment becomes an extension of the pedagogy. Augments traditional scoring across all CDI offerings starting 2027.

Train-the-Trainer Certification Pathway
How a campus professional goes from participant → certified CDI facilitator → institutional capacity that outlasts the partnership.
Certification Journey
1. Complete CDI Programs
Perspectives + Foundations + DFC — the existing offering sequence. Prerequisite for certification.
2. Trainer Curriculum
CDI-specific training for trainers — facilitation theory, common failure modes, quality standards, adaptation guidelines. What CDI facilitators know beyond the content.
3. Supervised Facilitation
Two-tier model: in-person observation with a CDI master facilitator for initial sessions, then AI-augmented remote assessment for ongoing quality assurance. Scales supervision without proportionally scaling CDI headcount.
4. CDI Certification
Certified CDI Facilitator credential. LinkedIn badge. Annual license. Authorized to deliver Foundations and DFC independently on their campus.
5. Recertification & QA
Annual recertification. Measurement data on their sessions feeds back to CDI. Quality control without CDI in the room. Recurring licensing revenue.
